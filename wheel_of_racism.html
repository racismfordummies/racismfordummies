The way that hair is treated in American culture is racist because certain beauty standards of what is “acceptable” are meant to suppress different cultures through hairstyles. For example, hair is a huge aspect of African American culture, but for decades, natural hair and traditionally black hairstyles have been regarded as “unprofessional” in the workplace, so African American women often style their hair in straight styles in order to conform to professional standards. Hair is used in situations like this to suppress unique cultures, especially those of people of color with “non-professional” hair types and styles.





Cultural appropriation in the name of “fashion” is an often blown-over, but prevalent problem in the U.S. While many cultures are regarded as less-than in America, certain aspects of that culture are sometimes stolen, and rebranded as some type of fad. This can be seen in makeup, when people of lighter complexions use makeup created for people with darker complexions or use fake tans in order to look darker, even though darker-skinned people have always been treated worse than their fairer counterparts in this nation. Cosmetic surgery also reflects this problem. While people of color have often been ridiculed for their fuller body-types and fuller lips, for example, now it has become one of the biggest trends to get plastic surgery in order to enhance such features. Clothing is also tainted by cultural appropriation, with cultural apparel and accessories often being rebranded as the “latest trend” without giving credit to the fact that this clothing has already existed within unique cultures.
