What Are Stereotypes?

Stereotypes. As we all know stereotypes are used to categorize certain groups of people, which overly simplifies the world to reduce the amount of thinking the comes into place as we meet new people. So stereotyping is a persons internalized way of categorizing or grouping individuals together, also known as having an 'unconscious bias'. Stereotyping is not limitied to only races, for example it can be used for gender, religions, regions and even weather. For example, if you have ever looked at the clouds outside and said, "it looks like its going to rain," If so, congatulations you have stereotyped the weather. However in some aspects, stereotypes can have some positive implications but they are typically use in a negative context.

Look at the chart below to see some pros and cons of stereotypes:

    PRO's

    Makes it easier to categorize cultures

    Provides a sense of belonging

    Able to learn from mistakes previously made

    CON's

    Enables prejudice/discriminatory ideals

    Generalizes/Strips people of their uniqueness
