Police Brutality

As defined by USLegal.com police brutality is, a civil rights violation that occurs when a police officer acts with excessive force by using an amount of force with regards to a civilian." Though this can occur people of all races, minorites are typically more often impacted by this. The "Dark Age of Police Brutality" showed rates of police brutality surge from 2014-Present. This can be represented by the cases of Eric Garner, Trayvon Martin, Freddie Gray, Sandra Bland, and the numerous other cases in which there have been no conviction for these cops using eccessive force against civilians which have lead their death.
